The trader's dictionary.

Plain-English definitions for every term used on this page. Click any term to expand.

Eval / Evaluation ▾
A paid trial period. You buy access to a simulated account, trade under published rules, and "pass" by hitting the profit target without violating the loss limits. Express Funding skips the eval — you start trading day one.
Simulated capital ▾
The $100K in the account is simulated, so you're not risking your savings to access it. The payouts earned against that simulated trading are real money, paid weekly.
Drawdown / Max drawdown ▾
The peak-to-trough loss in your account. Max drawdown is the largest loss allowed before the account is suspended. The trailing drawdown adjusts upward as you earn; once it reaches the high-water mark, it locks at your starting balance.
PDT rule (Pattern Day Trader) ▾
The old SEC rule requiring $25,000 minimum equity to day-trade four or more round trips in a five-business-day window. It has since been repealed and no longer restricts retail or funded day trading.
PFOF (Payment for Order Flow) ▾
When a retail broker sells your order flow to a market maker, who pays the broker for it — often filling with a spread slightly worse than direct institutional routing. Most commission-free brokers use PFOF; TradeFundrr's funded accounts don't.
Sharpe ratio ▾
A measure of risk-adjusted return. A high Sharpe means strong returns relative to volatility — the trader makes money without huge swings. T3 Global considers Sharpe (among other metrics) when reviewing graduates for live capital.
Profit split ▾
The percentage of profits you keep. TradeFundrr's split is 80/20: you keep 80%, the firm keeps 20%. Same across Growth, Express, and Pro.
Buying power ▾
The dollar value of stock you can hold intraday. A $25,000 balance with 4× leverage gives $100,000 of intraday buying power.
Audition path / Graduation ▾
The process by which a TradeFundrr trader earns a real-capital seat at T3 Global. Triggered by sustained Sharpe, disciplined drawdown, and consistent profitability over multiple months. Not automatic; reviewed case-by-case.

Should I start with Growth or Express?▾

If you want the lowest upfront cost and don't mind the hard-breach risk on the daily loss limit, start with Growth ($399 + $149 activation after passing the eval). If you want to skip the evaluation entirely, trade the funded account from day one, and have a softer safety net on rough days, choose Express ($899, $800 rebated on first qualifying payout).

Which means: Growth is cheaper but stricter. Express is pricier but more forgiving. Most experienced day traders pick Express for the soft breach alone.

What's a "hard breach" vs a "soft breach", and why does it matter?▾

Hit the daily loss limit ($1,000) on Growth and the current evaluation is suspended — you can pay a $99 reset to restart from Stage 1, or buy another $399 eval. That's a hard breach. On Express, hitting the same $1,000 just pauses the rest of that session; you come back the next day. Same account, no restart.

Which means: on Growth a bad day ends the current eval (but $99 buys another shot); on Express it costs you the rest of the session and that's it. For most active day traders, the soft breach justifies the $500 difference.

What does the 30% consistency rule actually mean for me?▾

Your largest single-session profit can't exceed 30% of your profit target. Target is $1,250 on both plans, so 30% = $375. Make more than $375 in one session and the target bumps up so that day represents 30% of the new total. Example: make $600 → new target = 600 / 0.30 = $2,000.

Which means: no single lucky day can pass you to a payout. You prove sustained edge over multiple sessions — trade steady rather than YOLO for a one-day pass.

How do the progressive payout caps work? When do they unlock?▾

First payout caps at $1,000 (Growth) or $2,000 (Express). Second: $1,500 / $2,500. Third: $2,000 / $3,500. After your third clean cycle, the cap unlocks to the full $25,000 max per request.

Which means: early payouts are deliberately smaller so the firm can confirm a real edge before sending bigger amounts. Three clean cycles and you're at the full $25K ceiling.

What's the difference between simulated funding (Growth/Express) and real capital (Pro Trader)?▾

Growth and Express use simulated funded accounts with $100K of simulated buying power; the payouts are real money against your simulated performance. Pro Trader is different — you contribute real capital (entry $100K+) and trade real-money buying power up to $20M+ through T3 Global's desk. No simulation, no evaluation, custom rules, real fills.

Which means: Growth/Express are for traders without big personal capital who want to prove themselves. Pro Trader is for traders who already have the edge and the capital to deploy.

Can I trade stocks, options, AND futures on one account?▾

The Growth and Express plans on this page are for stocks specifically. Options and futures have their own dedicated programs with similar mechanics but instrument-specific rules. Pro Trader Funding spans all three markets under one capital contribution.

Which means: for simulated funded accounts you sign up per market; for Pro Trader, one contribution gives multi-asset access.

If I pass Growth, can I "upgrade" to Express? Or move to Pro Trader?▾

Growth and Express are separate accounts, not tiers of the same one. Run them in parallel, or buy Express directly to skip the eval. Top performers on either plan get reviewed for the live-capital path through T3 Global — that's the real upgrade trajectory. Pro Trader is a separate decision based on your capital and profile.

Which means: no penalty for starting with Growth and adding Express later. The real long-term path is T3 Global live capital via sustained performance.

Can I really graduate to live capital at T3 Global?▾

Yes, but it's earned. Top performers with sustained Sharpe and disciplined drawdown audition for T3 Global's institutional desk. Not all traders qualify; the path is documented and open to those who demonstrate consistency over multiple months.

Which means: this isn't a marketing promise — there's a real audition path to real desk capital for traders who earn it. Pro Trader is the direct-entry version for those who already have the capital and edge.
