Program FAQs

What’s the profit target to get funded? +

$1,250 across all three programs. Hit it inside the published rules and you pass, with no time limit.

Which means: one clear bar to clear, the same for everyone, with no clock running against you.
How does the trailing drawdown work? +

Your max drawdown trails your balance upward, then locks at your initial balance ($3,750 on Stocks and Options, $2,500 on Futures). Once it locks, it stays put.

Which means: once you bank a cushion and it locks, your downside is fixed, not a moving target.
What is the consistency rule? +

You need 5 days of at least $250 in profit, and they don’t have to be consecutive. It rewards steady results over one lucky day.

Which means: you prove a repeatable process, not a one-day fluke, before you get funded.
Can I hold trades overnight or over the weekend? +

Swing and overnight trading isn’t allowed on Stocks or Options. Futures allows it during the week, but no weekend holds.

Which means: you always know exactly what you can hold, with no surprise rule changes.
Can I trade the news? +

Yes, news trading is allowed on all three programs. On Futures it carries a risk note given the added volatility.

Which means: you can trade your edge through real market events instead of sitting them out.
What if I don’t pass? Are resets free? +

Stocks and Options include 5 free resets, then $99 each. Futures includes 10 free resets, then $99 each.

Which means: one rough run doesn’t end your shot, you reset and go again.
How and when do I get paid? +

Payouts run weekly with an 80% profit split (minimum $250 per payout), paid from simulated profits straight to your account.

Which means: real money hits your account every week when you perform, not someday.
