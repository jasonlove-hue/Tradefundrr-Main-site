MECHANIC 01 Drawdown resets at first payout
Until your first payout: standard trailing safety line. After: the firm steps back. The line locks at start.

Every account starts with a trailing safety line: $3,000 below your starting balance on 50K accounts, $6,000 on 100K. As your account grows, the line trails up behind you each day. Standard prop-firm mechanic.

The moment you take your first weekly payout, the firm steps back. The line locks at your original starting balance and stops moving for the rest of the account’s life. From your second payout onward, you’re trading against a fixed floor, not a chasing one. You earned the room to manage your own risk. We trust you with it.

Which means Your first weekly payout permanently locks your safety line at your starting balance. After that, a winning week can’t pull your floor up behind you.
MECHANIC 02 30% consistency, forward-only
Big day above 30%? The firm steps back. The target nudges up. The rule never moves backward.

We fund traders, not gamblers. The 30% consistency rule says no single trading day can be more than 30% of your running profit target. Our way of making sure that everyone who clears a payout did it with disciplined trading, not a single oversized bet.

It applies once you’re funded. During the evaluation, Growth programs require steady gains (100% consistency); Express programs use the same 30% rule. Past evaluation, every program runs the same 30% standard.

Here’s where the firm steps back: if you do hit a big day above 30%, we don’t trim it out of your buffer. The profit target just steps up to absorb it. Your monster day still counts. The rule itself never moves backward.

Which means Your monster day still counts. The target nudges up to match. Nothing gets shaved off your buffer. The rule paces you, it doesn’t punish you.

MECHANIC 04 One warning per account
First breach: the firm steps back. Account pauses. Second breach: account closed.

Most prop firms close an account on the first daily loss limit hit. Here’s where the firm steps back: your first breach is a warning, not a death sentence. The account pauses, the trading day ends, and you resume the next morning with the account intact.

Hit the daily loss limit a second time on the same account and the account closes. One warning per account, ever.

A single ugly Tuesday morning shouldn’t end an account that had six clean weeks behind it. We trust you with one recoverable mistake. We’re funding human traders who occasionally have bad days, not algos that have to be perfect.

Which means Every account gets one mulligan. A bad Tuesday morning can’t end six clean weeks of trading.

Direct answers

Futures traders ask. We answer at the rule level.

No Lorem ipsum. No marketing dodges. The questions a sophisticated futures trader actually asks before buying — answered at the rule mechanic.

What’s the max drawdown rule? Is it trailing or static?

$3,000 (50K) / $6,000 (100K) EOD trailing. Calculated end-of-day, not intraday. The trailing watermark stops moving the moment you take your first payout and resets to your starting balance from that point forward. The firm steps out of the risk on cycle two onward.

How exactly does the consistency rule work?

30% consistency, post-funded only. No single trading session may exceed 30% of your running profit target. If it does, the profit target recalculates upward to keep you within the band — the rule itself doesn’t move retroactively. Evaluation phase: 100% consistency on Growth, 30% on Express.

What’s the breach mechanic?

1 soft → hard. First daily-loss breach in the account’s lifetime pauses the account, ends the session, account resumes the next trading day. Second daily-loss breach in the same account: immediate hard breach, account closed. One warning per account, ever.

Minimum days to first payout?

10 trading days funded. The 10-day minimum applies on every program (Growth, Express, Promo). Subsequent payouts run on the weekly cycle once you’re past the 10-day minimum and have hit the per-cycle threshold.

Per-cycle payout caps vs lifetime caps — how do they interact?

Per-cycle is your withdrawal ceiling per week: $1K (50K) / $1.5K (100K) on cycles 1-3, rising to $1.5K / $3K on cycle 4+. Lifetime is the account close trigger: $15K (Growth) / $25K (Express) cumulative across that account. Hit the lifetime cap, the account closes; you continue on your other accounts.

News trading? Algos? Hold time?

News trading: yes, on every program, no restrictions. Algos: no, manual trading only. Minimum hold time: >15 seconds. Same rules across Growth, Express, and Promo.

What’s the live capital path?

When you trade consistently after your first or second payout, you graduate to T3 Global with real capital. $2,000 per 50K, $3,000 per 100K, up to 5 accounts ($10K-$15K total backing). Defined the day you sign up. Allocated at graduation.

Why T3 Global?

The T3 group of companies includes a FINRA/SIPC regulated SEC broker-dealer (T3 Trading Group) and an institutional trading firm where TradeFundrr’s funded accounts execute (T3 Global). Regulated, operational depth — the difference between a payout you can count on and one that depends on next month’s sign-ups.
