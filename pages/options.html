If you fail Stage 1 or Stage 2, you can pay a $99 reset fee to restart the evaluation. The reset takes you back to Stage 1, any progress made in Stage 2 doesn't carry over.

▾Daily loss breach typeHard breach

Growth uses a hard breach. Hit the daily loss limit and the account is suspended for the rest of the evaluation. You can pay the $99 reset fee to restart from Stage 1, or buy another full evaluation.

Which means: a hard breach ends the current eval, but the $99 reset is a much cheaper second-chance than buying another $399 evaluation. You do lose earned progress when you reset.

▾Max end-of-day drawdown$3,000

Trailing drawdown calculated on your highest end-of-day equity. Hit $3,000 below peak and the account is suspended.

Which means: keep your peak-to-trough EOD swing inside $3,000 and you keep trading. As you earn, the trailing drawdown rises with your equity until it locks at the high-water mark.

▾Min trade duration15 seconds

Trades must be held for at least 15 seconds.

Which means: scalping is fine; pure HFT-style sub-second flips aren't allowed. 15 seconds is a low bar, matches most retail day-trading workflows.

▾Consistency rule30%

Your largest single-session profit can't exceed 30% of your profit target. If it does, the profit target adjusts upward so that session represents only 30%.

Example: profit target is $1,250 (30% = $375). If you make $600 in one session, the new target becomes 600 / 0.30 = $2,000.

Which means: no single lucky day can pass you. You need to demonstrate consistency over multiple sessions to qualify for payouts.

▾Payout requirement5 days @ $250

Which means: 5 disciplined winning days at $250+ each unlocks your payout eligibility. Combined with the consistency rule, this filters for sustained edge over fluke wins.

The trader's dictionary.

Plain-English definitions for every term used on this page. Click any term to expand:

Eval / Evaluation▾
A paid trial period. You buy access to a simulated account, trade under published rules, and "pass" the evaluation by hitting the profit target without violating the loss limits. Express Funding skips the eval, you start trading day one.
Simulated capital▾
The $25K options account is simulated, so you're not risking your savings to access it. The payouts earned against that simulated trading are real money, paid weekly to your Rise account.
Drawdown / Max drawdown▾
The peak-to-trough loss in your account. Max drawdown is the largest loss allowed before the account is suspended. The trailing drawdown adjusts upward as you earn; once it reaches the high-water mark, it locks at your starting balance.
PDT rule (Pattern Day Trader)▾
The old SEC rule requiring $25,000 minimum equity to day-trade four or more round trips in a five-business-day rolling window. It has since been repealed and no longer restricts retail or funded day trading.
PFOF (Payment for Order Flow)▾
When a retail broker sells your order flow to a market maker, who pays the broker for it. The market maker fills your order, often with a spread slightly worse than direct institutional routing. Most retail commission-free brokers use PFOF; TradeFundrr's funded accounts don't.
Sharpe ratio▾
A measure of risk-adjusted return. A high Sharpe means strong returns relative to volatility, i.e. the trader makes money without huge swings. T3 Global considers Sharpe (alongside other metrics) when reviewing TradeFundrr graduates for live capital.
Profit split▾
The percentage of profits you keep. TradeFundrr's split is 80/20: you keep 80%, the firm keeps 20%. Same across Growth, Express, and Pro.
Buying power▾
The dollar value of options exposure you can hold intraday. On TradeFundrr options accounts, exposure is gated by the contract limit (10 for Growth, 20 for Express) rather than a raw buying-power figure.
Audition path / Graduation▾
The process by which a TradeFundrr trader earns a real-capital seat at T3 Global. Triggered by sustained Sharpe, disciplined drawdown, and consistent profitability over multiple months. Not automatic; reviewed and approved on a case-by-case basis.
