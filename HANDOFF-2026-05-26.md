## Key Design Decisions

### "Forces away from Bose" — what we did and why

Nadav's feedback: the Christian slide forced the reader's attention AWAY from Christian to the partnership block. Diagnosed cause: the lead and bio both explicitly redirected ("credibility doesn't come from his resume... it comes from the partnerships listed below"). Fix: rewrite both to give Christian agency in the building of TradeFundrr without overpromising customer-facing work he doesn't do. Partnership block retained (Nadav framework: face + infrastructure) but tightened so it doesn't dominate the bio's visual weight.

### Payout rebate — keep mechanic, soften language

Two business mechanics were on the table:
- **A) Success-refund** (current offer): you pass eval + earn first payout → you get $800 back. Just safer language with mail-in friction.
- **B) Fail-refund** (Nadav's literal words): "if you don't hit your payout within X days, submit a form and we'll mail you $X back." Different product entirely.

Jason chose **A** — keep current offer, no mail-in form, just legally cleaner language. The headline retains marketing punch ("Hit your first payout. Get your eval fee back."); the supporting sub, points, and footnote carry the non-guarantee language and per-plan specifics.

### Options hero headline differentiation

Stocks headline: "Use your edge. / Not your $25K."

For options, the binding retail constraint isn't account size — it's contract count. Jason picked "Scale your edge. / Not your risk." from a set of 10 options. Active verb opening (`Scale`) + reassurance closer (`risk`). Different rhythm from stocks but same theme.

### Open-in-new-tab as default

Jason direction: every link click should open in a new tab. Applied to all navigational links sitewide. In-page scroll anchors stay same-tab (target="_blank" on a scroll-link would jump to a new tab and scroll there — undesired). `mailto:`/`tel:`/`javascript:` skipped since target doesn't change their behavior.

## Recurring Risks (lessons from today)

### OneDrive null-byte truncation

Hit multiple times today on both `pages/options.html` and `assets/css/mobile-fixes.css`. Symptoms: file ends with hundreds of `\x00` bytes after the real content, sometimes the real content tail is also truncated.

Fix pattern that worked:
1. Read file as bytes, `data.rstrip(b"\x00")` to strip null tail
2. If file ends mid-content (e.g., cut at "movi" mid-CSS-keyword), pull the missing tail from a parallel file (e.g., `pages/stocks.html` has the same JS block as `pages/options.html`) and append
3. Always use atomic writes: `tempfile.mkstemp` + `os.replace` to prevent partial writes

### Em dashes in body text are forbidden

Per ongoing convention: no em dashes (`—`) in visible body text. Dev comments are fine. Every Python script that injects new copy runs a pre-flight `assert chr(0x2014) not in new_content`. Hyphen, comma, period, or colon depending on context.

### Preview banner convention

When creating preview HTML files (e.g., for Nadav review), every section under review gets a banner inside the section:
```html
<div style="background:#22d3a8;color:#060914;text-align:center;padding:10px 16px;font-family:'Geist Mono',monospace;font-size:11px;letter-spacing:1.8px;text-transform:uppercase;font-weight:600;margin-bottom:24px;border-radius:6px;">
  Review · [Section name] · [Brief context]
</div>
```

Banners must be stripped before porting to production.

### PowerShell multi-line commit messages

PowerShell's `git commit -m "..."` does NOT handle embedded newlines like bash. Multi-line commit messages cause PowerShell to enter continuation mode (`>>` prompts). Jason hit this today. Workaround: use a single-line `-m` message, or PowerShell here-string syntax `@"..."@`. For complex history, commit with one short `-m`, then `git commit --amend` for more detail.

### Atomic file writes for OneDrive

Always: `tempfile.mkstemp(suffix=".html", dir=os.path.dirname(path))` + write + `os.replace(tmp, path)`. Avoids OneDrive's mid-write file truncation pattern. Direct `open(path, "w")` is the wrong tool here.
